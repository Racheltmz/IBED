Dr Reza Shah-Kazemi

Dr Reza Shah-Kazemi (b. 1 June, 1960) is an author who specializes in comparative mysticism, Islamic Studies, Sufism and Shi'ism. He is the founding editor of the Islamic World Report and currently a Research Associate at the Institute of Ismaili Studies with the Department of Academic Research and Publications. He received degrees in International Relations and Politics at Sussex and Exeter University, before receiving his doctorate in Comparative Religion from the University of Kent in 1994. He later acted as a consultant to the Institute for Policy Research in Kuala Lumpur.

Dr Reza was the author of "Common Ground Between Islam and Buddhism", published in 2010, whereby the foreword to the book was written by His Holiness the Dalai Lama.
